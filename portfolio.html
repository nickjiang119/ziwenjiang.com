hEDGE Financial Conference

Print and Graphics Design

November 21, 2019

Graphic Design - Delegate package for all attendees and sponsors at hEDGE Financial Conference 2019.

Overview

what is Hedge?

hEDGE Financial Conference is Canada's largest student investment banking and finance conference hosted in Toronto. In 2019, I was contracted to re-design their branding, promotional products and packages. The project took 2 months to complete with numerous iterations and changes to meet my client's needs.

Challenge

The organization was looking for a complete brand re-vamp from a traditional business conference to a modern style. As the project lead, I chose to implement a shift to a flat design style leveraging key elements and shapes from the original logo and colours.

Deliverables

Delegate Package

The delegate package was designed to highlight hEDGE's origin city: Toronto. I re-created flat designs of key landmarks to use as background images. To keep readers engaged with content, backgrounds were faded out while important information was clearly sectioned with highlight colours.

Previous Next

Hedge 2019 Delegate Package - All pages

Posters and Banners

Graphical assets were created for marketing usage and designed for both print and web distribution. Print posters (A4) and Stand-up Banner (80cm x 200cm) graphics were created to meet specifications for print. All graphics were designed using a combination of Sketch and Adobe Photoshop . Photoshop was used to create the background assets and buildings while Sketch was used for placement and overall layout design.

Collection of Facebook Banners, Stand-up banner, A4 Print Poster and Social Media graphics.

Conclusion

Over the course of this project, I was tasked with the redesign and rebranding of a organization shifting to meet modern design standards. Through incorporating the original style guide with applications of minimal flat-design graphics, I was able to help build an all-inclusive brand experience that felt new but still retained the key elements that made hEDGE identifiable.

Created graphics were used both in print and for online marketing. Printed booklets were distributed to all 250 attendees at the event and social media posts reached a cumulative audience of over 20,000 views.

Overall, I learnt a lot about the theory and application of print design and social media design principles. I had a great time working with the hEDGE organizing team and look forward to collaborating again for their event in 2020.

Google Material Design Analysis

Blog Post

November 12, 2019

Google Design video exploring the foundation and principles of Material Design.
source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rrT6v5sOwJg&t=3s

Overview

what is Material Design?

Material Design, as defined by Material.io, is:

" A visual language that synthesizes the classic principles of good design with the innovation of technology and science. "

- Material.io, 2019

In simpler terms, Material design is a design language created by Google to enrich an user's on-screen touch experience through vivid ques, 3D effects, and realistic shades that mimic real-world objects. Material design is a relatively new concept that has quickly grown roots in many designs today.

While researching the subject, I found this very interesting video on the Google Design channel that explores how Material Design was born and the role that it plays for designers at Google. Please watch the video above if the subject interests you, I promise it's a worthwhile view.

Analysis

If you don't want to sit through 7 minutes of footage, I've prepared a short summary of the video:

Material design was born from a clash between multi-device design and user experience resistance to depth perception. Designers at Google wanted to communicate surface and depth within their designs across all platforms to encourage users to easily identify and engage with key elements such as buttons, applications and more.

The Floating Action Button or FAB is one of the most simple but iconic examples of Google's Material Design:

When pressed, FAB buttons would visibaly depressed or lifted when pressed. By creating elements that felt real and gave visual feedback when interacted with, users could feel like they were really pressing down on a physical button. In addition, the creators at Google included harmonious colour schemes and simple sans-serif fonts and to create a living, natural-feeling ecosystem of user interaction and design.

Conclusion

Overall, Material Design has enabled even non-designers to be able to create visually appealing platforms and systems with ease. It has dramatically changed the way that users innately interact with a website, a form and so much more. The question is, what is to come and what can future designers can create using Material Design as a foundation.

Hack the 6ix

Isometric Graphic Design

October 29, 2019

Facebook and website banner for Hack the 6ix 2019.

Overview

what is Hack the 6ix?

Hack the 6ix is my own organization that I founded. For five years now, Hack the 6ix has been Toronto's largest hackathon that takes place every summer for 36 hours straight, in the heart of downtown Toronto.

For those unfamiliar, a hackathon is a:

" A gathering where programmers collaboratively code in an extreme manner over a short period of time, usually a few days. While working on a particular project, the idea is for each developer to have the ability and freedom to work on whatever he/she wants."

- Techopedia, 2019

Challenge

As the Chair and head of the organization, I collaborated closely with my internal design team to customally design unique social media graphics, website designs and isometric graphics as part of our brand initative. Our goal was to re-invent our brand to communicate openess, cool brand ascethics and technology empowerment of local Toronto tech firms. To accomplish our goal, I helped create numerous graphics and wireframes throughout the year.

Deliverables

Sponsor Isometric Buildings

I recreated several isometric buildings for the sponsors of our event. Large corporations often seek out hackathons as a prime location for recruitment of tech talent. As such, we wanted to creatively acknowledge our sponsors while also offering them a small custom souvenir for supporting our cause. All buildings were created based on the real HQ buildings of our respective sponsors using a combination of Sketch Illustrator and Photoshop.

Collection of Facebook Banners, Stand-up banner, A4 Print Poster and Social Media graphics.

Conclusion

Over the course of this project, I helped transition my organization through a major brand change. While the effort was immense, we managed to complete a custom design for each of our 23 sponsors.

If you are interested in learning more about Hack the 6ix or even wish to participate next year, please check out Hack the 6ix for more info!

Biomimicry

Blog Post

October 16, 2019

Vox video exploring the roots of nature's influence in modern objects and design.
source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iMtXqTmfta0

Overview

what is Biomimicry?

Biomimicry, as defined by Google, is:

" The design and production of materials, structures, and systems that are modeled on biological entities and processes. "

- Google, 2019

iomimicry is the science of applying nature-inspired designs in human engineering and invention to solve human problems. It was used to create the first flying machine, inspired by eagles and owls—this paved the way for technologies like jets and planes.

Other examples include the Velco which was inspired by the hooks used by burrs to latch onto animals. Or Japan’s Sanyo Shinkansen bullet train inspired by owls, kingfishers and penguins as explored in the video.

Please give it a watch!

Analysis

If you don't want to sit through 7 minutes of footage, I've prepared a short summary of the video:

Biomimicry can be found all around us from achitecture, to health care, to transportation. One such example is Japan's Sanyo Shinkansen bullet train, the fastest trains in the world. Earlier models originally faced noise issues due to massive wind flows accumulating in-front when in tunnels. To solve this, engineers studied the areodynamics of several birds to reduce air resistance.

After careful research, the engineers redesigned the train with new designs based on the wings of a owl, the belly of a penguin and the beak of a kingfisher for various components of the train. This helped reduce energy costs by 15%, increase speed by 10% while lowering noise to less than 70 decibals. The Shinkansen is just one of the many examples how modern day design constantly draws inspiration from nature.

Conclusion

Biomimicry is an amazing principle of design, when exploring material design, flat design or product design, it is possible to draw insights from plants and animals to improve effiency and enhance utility.

I am excited to see what the future holds in store as more and more technologies and designs incorporate nature's principles.
